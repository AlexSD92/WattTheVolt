Back on the Bandwagon

It’s been a minute since I last posted.

After finishing up my family holiday in Spain, I found it hard to carve out time to properly sit down and go through my electrician training lectures. I had to return early from the trip to go back to work, and then a week later I was off to Spain again — this time for a bachelor party with friends. Needless to say, progress was delayed.

But I’ve picked it back up over the last couple of weeks. I’ve trooped through most of the lecture content now and have about 10 remaining. That said, because it’s been a while between starting the course and where I am now, I can already see myself revisiting the lectures again when I start digging into the books and taking the trial exams. It’s not a problem — except for the time commitment.

Something I’ve noticed about myself: I have a bad habit of trying to run before I can walk. I want to be good at something now. I expect myself to “get it” quickly — and when I don’t, I start criticising myself. What’s strange is, I’d never say that to someone else. I’d never think someone else was taking “too long” to learn a skill. That pressure is something I aim squarely at myself.

The other day, though, something helped me put it in perspective.

I was out with my daughter while she rode her bike, and I was just in awe of how far she’d come. I was watching her shift gears and push her pace, and it hit me: she’s come such a long way in such a short time. I bought the bikes in April 2025 — so it's been about five months of consistent practice, maybe 3–4 rides a week.

That’s five months from “don’t let go, Dad” to “go faster!” She’s still a beginner — don’t get me wrong — but a confident one. Watching her made me realise how powerful steady effort really is. We often overlook the slow grind in favour of the big leap. But it’s the slow grind that builds skill.

I need to offer myself that same patience. I’m still early in my journey, and it’s okay to take the long route, as long as I keep moving.

So yes — this is my first post in a while. If anyone out there has been reading and wondering where I went — thanks for sticking around. I’m back now, and hopefully for good. Consider this a soft reboot and me getting back on the bandwagon.

Weekly posts resume now.
