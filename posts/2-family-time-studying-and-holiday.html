Balancing Study, Family, and Sunshine

This is perhaps my first real time-management challenge. I’ve been on the course for a couple of weeks now and we’re starting to cover the different types of domestic circuit arrangements—things like TN-C, TN-S, and TT systems. It’s a lot to take in at once, especially without the chance to get hands-on. You’re mostly just looking at wiring configurations and being asked to identify the system, which I’d definitely remember better if I were building one myself. It’s made me realise just how much I’m looking forward to the practical workshop sessions.

To explain briefly:

  • TN-S (Terra Neutral Separate): Earth and neutral conductors are separate all the way through. Often used in older installations or where a dedicated earth is required.
  • TN-C (Terra Neutral Combined): Neutral and earth are combined into one conductor—the PEN conductor—for the whole system.
  • TN-C-S (Protective Multiple Earthing or PME): Neutral and earth are combined in the supply but split at the installation. This is the most common setup in the UK today.
  • TT (Terra Terra): Earth comes from a local electrode at the property, independent of the supply. Often found in rural areas or older properties.

The theory’s genuinely interesting—I’m into physics and I love learning how things work—but I’m keen to start making real circuits with my hands.

This week, I’m flying to Malaga, Spain with my partner and our two little girls to visit my mum and dad. I actually grew up there and still have a lot of family in the area, so it’s more than just a holiday—it’s a return to roots. I’ve got great memories of the place, and I’m really looking forward to getting daily ice creams, going out for food, and playing around in the pool with the kids.

The course doesn’t stop though. The lectures I need to get through before practical training are around 3 hours long, and there are quite a few of them. My goal is to complete at least one a day while I’m away. Ideally, I’d like to get the practical side done before Christmas, but it requires five consecutive weekends, so I need to plan carefully.

I’ve felt a bit of anxiety creeping in. Last time I did a bootcamp-style course, I pushed myself way too hard. I tied my success in the program to getting a job afterward, and even though I smashed the course, I struggled with job hunting. That led to more hours at the computer, less time with my kids, and a growing sense of frustration and burnout. It became a loop—burned out from effort, frustrated by lack of results, and disconnected from the people I care about most.

This time, though, things feel different. I’m still ambitious, but I’ve got a better support system. My partner’s incredible—she helps me find time and buys me space when I need it. If I’m overwhelmed, she helps me plan. She sees where I’m struggling and steps in—not to fix it for me, but to help me fix it myself. That kind of support is gold, and I’m grateful.

I’ll be checking in again while I’m away. My aim is to complete five lectures and post once to this blog within the first week. I also want to strike a healthy balance—enough time with my family, and enough progress in my training to keep momentum. If I can manage both, I’ll consider it a win.
